  Tonal/exposure
  - Brightness / Exposure
  - Contrast
  - Highlights & Shadows (separate control)
  - Blacks & Whites (separate clipping points)
  - Curves (the generalization of all of the above)
  - Levels (input/output black, white, midtone — already in RasterLab)

  Color
  - Saturation / Vibrance
  - Hue shift
  - Color Balance (shadows/mids/highlights)
  - Temperature & Tint (white balance)
  - HSL panel (per-hue hue/saturation/luminance)

  Geometry
  - Crop (already in RasterLab)
  - Rotate / Straighten (already in RasterLab)
  - Flip horizontal / vertical
  - Perspective / Keystone correction
  - Resize / Resample

  Local/effects
  - Vignette (already in RasterLab)
  - Blur (Gaussian)
  - Sharpen / Unsharp Mask (already in RasterLab)
  - Noise reduction
  - Grain / Film noise addition

  Conversion
  - Black & White (already in RasterLab)
  - Sepia tone
  - Color grading / LUT application

  Output
  - JPEG/PNG export with quality settings (already in RasterLab)
  - Resize on export
  - Color space conversion (sRGB → P3, etc.)

  RasterLab currently covers: Crop, Rotate, Sharpen, B&W, Levels, Vignette, JPEG/PNG export.

  The highest-value gaps are probably Brightness/Contrast, Curves, Saturation, and Flip — those are the things users reach for constantly in
  any photo editor.
